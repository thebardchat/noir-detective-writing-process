# You Probably Think This Book Is About You

> *It was always about you. It was never only about you.*

---

A two-volume collection of noir-style vignettes about ego, identity, and the universal human condition. Written in third person. Dark but light. Cynical but comical.

## The Process

This book was written using a pipeline that doesn't exist in any writing manual:

**Shane talks. Claude shapes.**

Raw voice dumps — recorded while driving, walking through hardware stores, ordering crabby patties at drive-throughs, scanning groceries — get transcribed and then pressed into noir prose. The detective's world is built from real observations dictated in real time on real roads in north Alabama.

The AI doesn't invent the stories. The AI finds the story inside the noise and gives it the cadence it was already reaching for.

### What a raw voice dump looks like

A raw dump is messy. It's fragments and tangents and background noise and the sound of a man thinking out loud while the road unspools in front of him. It might switch languages mid-sentence. It might pause to order food. It might describe a cemetery dog and a Bradford pear tree and the word "conscience" all in the same breath.

The dump is the material. The shaping is the craft. Neither works without the other.

### What comes out the other side

Noir third person. Short punches followed by long runs. No names — the detective, the old man, the garbage man, the small string one. You know them. You've always known them.

The voice is specific but the people are universal. That's the trick. You read it and think it's about someone you know. Then you realize it's about you. Then you realize it's about everyone. Then you realize the title told you that from the beginning.

## The Stack

This entire project runs on:

- **Raspberry Pi 5** (16GB RAM) with Pironman 5-MAX case
- **Claude** (Anthropic) — the co-writer, the shaping engine
- **ElevenLabs** — audiobook voice generation
- **A truck with a rattling cupholder** — the recording studio

No cloud compute. No writing retreats. No MFA. Just a Pi, an AI, and a man who notices things on his drive home.

## Two Volumes

### Volume One — *You Probably Think This Book Is About You*
22 scenes. A detective who doesn't know he's the case. The world observed from behind a steering wheel, through gas station fluorescence, past garbage men who don't look up. First draft complete. ~12,800 words. 78-minute audiobook.

### Volume Two — *You Probably Think This Song Is About You Too*
The sequel that isn't a sequel. Structured as an album — tracks, sides, grooves. The detective isn't investigating anymore. He's listening. The record keeps playing whether you flip it or not.

In progress. The album tells you what it is.
