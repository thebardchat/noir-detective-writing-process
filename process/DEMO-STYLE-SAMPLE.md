# Style Demo

> **This is NOT from the book.** This is an original piece written specifically for this repo to demonstrate the voice and cadence. It does not appear in either volume.

---

The parking lot had three cars in it and one of them was his and one of them was wrong.

Not wrong in the broken sense. Wrong in the way that a thing is wrong when it's parked in the spot you always park in even though the spot is not yours and has never been yours and the painted lines make no promises and yet. And yet. The spot was his the way the drive was his and the cupholder rattle was his and the Tuesday was his and the man in the wrong spot had taken something without knowing he'd taken it because the taking was invisible and the invisible things are the ones that rearrange the day.

He parked one spot over.

One spot over is a compromise. One spot over is the adult version of the thing the child wanted to say but didn't because the child learned — somewhere between the waving and the not-waving — that saying the thing costs more than the spot is worth.

He got out.

Locked the truck.

Walked past the wrong car without looking at it.

That's what detectives do. They notice everything. They look at nothing.

---

*This demonstrates: noir third person, short punches + long runs, dry humor, the detective's observation-without-judgment, no names, the cupholder callback, the cadence alternation.*
